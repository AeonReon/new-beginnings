Books & Reading

The books a child hears at three and four become the furniture of their mind. So it's worth being fussy about which ones get in.

Human stories for human children

Open most modern children's books and you'll find talking animals — ducks who go to school, bears with feelings, rabbits who won't nap. A little of it is charming. But a child's world is almost entirely made of it, and that's odd, because our children are not ducks or ants. They are small humans, learning to be human.

A story is how a young child rehearses being a person — courage, kindness, fear, friendship, loss, home. Human stories let them try those on directly, in bodies like their own. You can't cut every animal out, and you needn't. But a real share of what they hear should be humans doing human things, so the mirror they're handed actually reflects them.

First draft — let's shape this together. Below is a starting handful of genuinely good, mostly-human children's books to react to. This is exactly the kind of list that needs your eye, not an auto-generated pile. Tell me what lands and what doesn't and we'll build the real shelf.

Where we'd start

The Alfie stories Human

Shirley Hughes

Ordinary British family life through a small boy's eyes — the lost shoe, the friend's party, the new baby. Warm, real, beautifully drawn. Hughes is the gold standard for everyday human stories.

Dogger Human

Shirley Hughes

A boy loses his beloved toy and his big sister saves the day. Loss, love between siblings, and a small act of sacrifice — the whole emotional world of a four-year-old in one book.

Katie Morag stories Human

Mairi Hedderwick

A wee girl on a Scottish island, her two grandmothers, the post, the tides. Close to home for a NI family — real places, real work, a strong human world with no talking animals in sight.

The Snowy Day & Peter's Chair Human

Ezra Jack Keats

A small boy and the simple wonders of a city childhood — footprints in snow, the arrival of a baby sister. Quiet, timeless, deeply human.

Ox-Cart Man & Miss Rumphius Human

Barbara Cooney / Donald Hall

The turning year of a working family, and a woman who sets out to make the world more beautiful. Handsome, calm, and full of the shape of a good human life.

A Chair for My Mother Human

Vera B. Williams

A girl, her mum and her gran save coins in a jar for one good armchair after a fire. Family, work, hope and love — nothing sentimental, everything true.

Old tales, myths & the good fairy stories

Retellings — Grimm, Greek myths (D'Aulaires), folk tales

The oldest human stories we have. Chosen well and not over-sweetened, they carry courage, cunning, justice and wonder — the moral imagination, handed down. (We'll pick the gentler ones for four.)

Audiobooks & listening

Good listening counts as reading at this age — it builds vocabulary and attention while hands are busy or the car is moving.

LibriVox (librivox.org) reads out-of-copyright classics for free. We'll add a short list of the best human-story audiobooks — and, as more like-minded people start making their own, this is a shelf that should get a lot richer over the next few years.
