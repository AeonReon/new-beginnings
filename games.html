Games

Little out-loud games to play in the car, the garden, or while you wait — no screen, nothing to buy, nothing to set up. Just you, your child, and the turn going back and forth. Each one says the real thing it's building.

How to use this

You don't need all of these, and you don't need to do them "right". Pick one that fits where you are — stuck in traffic, walking to the shop, waiting for food — and play until it stops being fun. Most are turn-taking round games: you go, they go, round and round until someone runs out.

Every game carries the actual reason it helps — the specific skill and how the game rehearses it — not "because it's educational". A four-year-old's real work is play and talk; these turn ordinary in-between moments into the richest learning there is, without a single worksheet. Trust your child's appetite over any list: if a game bores them, drop it and try another.
