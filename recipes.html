Recipes

Nice things to make with your child — snacks, dinners and puddings built from real food, with none of the sugar-loads, E-numbers and additives the shop versions carry. All low-skill, all quick. The child who helps make it is the child who eats it.

Why cook it together

Most food aimed at children is engineered to sell, not to nourish — sugar to make it moreish, cheap oils and fillers to make it cheap, colours and preservatives to make it last. A child raised on it grows a taste for it.

The fix isn't a lecture, it's a countertop. When a child mashes the banana, rolls the ball, or arranges their own toppings, three things happen at once: they eat what they made, they learn where food comes from, and their taste quietly trains toward the real thing. Every recipe here says the actual reason it beats the shop version — no "because it's healthy", the real swap.
