Health & Body

A calm, first-principles take on a child's health — the things that actually move the needle at this age, and the confidence to tell the small stuff from the stuff that needs a doctor.

Being built now. Here's what this pillar will hold — each with the real reason behind it, same as everything else in the app. Not medical advice; a thinking parent's starting point. Anything worrying always goes to your GP.

😴 Sleep

What a four-year-old's sleep actually needs, why the bedtime battle happens, and the rhythm that ends it — without cry-it-out dogma or clock-watching.

Sleep is when the growing and the wiring happen. Get it right and half the day's "behaviour" problems quietly disappear.

🏃 Movement & outdoors

How much a young body needs to move, why "hyper" is usually under-moved, and why time outside does more than any indoor activity.

A child's nervous system is built by motion, climbing, swinging and rough-and-tumble. It's not surplus energy to burn off — it's construction.

📱 Screens

A clear-eyed look at what screens do to a developing brain, sleep and attention at this age — and a workable line to hold that isn't all-or-nothing.

The early years build attention and imagination from boredom and real play. Fast screens train the opposite; the cost is paid later.

🦷 Teeth & the everyday body

The small daily things that save big trouble — teeth, hydration, fresh air, sunlight — and the simple version of each.

Most childhood health is habit, not intervention. The boring daily basics outperform almost everything else.

🌡️ When to worry, when to wait

A calm guide to the common ones — fevers, coughs, tummy bugs, rashes — what's usually fine to ride out, and the red flags that mean phone someone now.

Most illness at this age is the immune system doing its job. Knowing the genuine warning signs lets you stay calm for the ninety percent that isn't serious.
